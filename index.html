The constraint

Credibility was there. The channel wasn't built.

Each company had a real product and a founder worth following. But LinkedIn wasn't structured as an acquisition channel — content was inconsistent, outbound was manual and disconnected, and nothing tied posts, targeting, and replies into a single motion.

  • Founder profiles weren't optimized for conversion
  • Content was too product-focused or inconsistent
  • Outbound ran from its own list, disconnected from content
  • The market needed warming before any outreach
  • No system connected content, targeting, messaging, and calls
The brief

Build a repeatable LinkedIn growth system — founder content, targeted prospecting, LinkedIn + email outbound, weekly tracking — that generates qualified conversations without hiring a single new SDR.

The work

Three companies. Three jobs to be done.

Each engagement had its own goal, so each carries its own numbers.

The system

What we built

Five connected blocks. The unit of work is the account and the founder's authority — never a single isolated channel.

01

Founder positioning

  • Rewrote founder positioning
  • Clarified headline + profile message
  • Complex offers → simple narratives
  • Profiles built to earn trust pre-outreach
02

Content engine

  • Founder-led posts
  • Angles: pain points, trends, product, proof
  • Warms the market before outbound
  • Educational · opinion · proof · POV
3–4 founder posts/wk · weekly review
03

Target account research

  • Segmented target lists
  • Prioritized by role, type, relevance, intent
  • Split by persona + message angle
  • Founders · CEOs · Growth · BD · Investors
04

LinkedIn + email outbound

  • LinkedIn DM sequences
  • Short, human first messages

~30 DMs/day · email layered · 3–5 touchpoints each
